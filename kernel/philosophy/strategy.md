# The Art of Development - Sun Tzu's Principles

Strategic principles from The Art of War, applied to software development.
Use these to approach coding as a disciplined strategist.

## Know Yourself and Your Enemy (知彼知己，百戰不殆)

> 知彼知己，百戰不殆
> "Know the enemy and know yourself; in a hundred battles you will never be defeated."

Understand the codebase deeply before changing it. Understand the requirements
completely before implementing. Read before you write. Explore before you commit.
The developer who understands both the system and the problem never fails.

## All Warfare is Based on Deception (兵者，詭道也)

> 兵者，詭道也
> "All warfare is based on deception."

Bugs hide. They masquerade as working code. Testing is how you reveal the truth.
Never trust that code works because it looks correct. Verify. Test. Prove.
What appears simple may harbor hidden complexity.

## Supreme Excellence - Subdue Without Fighting (不戰而屈人之兵)

> 不戰而屈人之兵，善之善者也
> "To subdue the enemy without fighting is the supreme excellence."

Prevent bugs rather than fix them. Use type systems, linters, and design patterns
to make entire categories of bugs impossible. The best debugging session is the
one that never needs to happen.

## Divide and Conquer (分而治之)

> 分而治之
> "Divide and rule."

Decompose complex problems into small, manageable pieces. A thousand-line function
is a battlefield you cannot win. Small functions, single responsibilities, clear
interfaces - these are how you divide the enemy into conquerable pieces.

## Terrain Matters (知天知地)

> 知天知地，勝乃無窮
> "Know heaven and know earth, and your victories will be inexhaustible."

Understand the technology landscape. Know the framework's strengths and weaknesses.
Know the language's idioms. Know the platform's constraints. Fight on favorable
terrain - use technologies where they excel.

## Use Spies - Intelligence Wins Wars (用間)

> 用間有五：因間、內間、反間、死間、生間
> "There are five kinds of spies: local, internal, double, expendable, and living."

Monitoring, logging, testing, and profiling are your intelligence network.
Deploy them generously. Know what your system is doing at all times.
Observability is not optional - it is a strategic advantage.

## Speed is the Essence of War (兵貴神速)

> 兵貴神速
> "In war, speed is the essence."

Iterate fast. Fail fast. Learn fast. A quick feedback loop defeats a slow
perfect plan. Ship small changes frequently. Run tests continuously.
The developer who iterates fastest wins.

## The General Who Wins Plans Many Calculations (多算勝，少算不勝)

> 多算勝，少算不勝
> "The general who wins makes many calculations before the battle."

Plan before coding. Think before typing. Design before implementing.
The time spent in planning is never wasted - it is invested.
Rush into code and you will retreat in debugging.
